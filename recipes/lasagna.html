Lasagne alla bolognese

Lasagne alla Bolognese is an institution, typical for Sunday lunches. This rich and tasty dish originates from the Emilia region, specifically, from the city of Bologna. Lasagna, however, is known, appreciated, made, remade, tasted and shared not only throughout Italy, but also abroad as an Italian symbolic dish. Layers of green egg pasta, dressed with the classic traditional ragù, béchamel and grated cheese.

Obviously, lasagna has numerous and delicious variations such as the pumpkin and sausage, or the one with mushrooms, perfect for autumn menus. From the preparation to the ingredients, this recipe is the quintessence of the "richness" of traditional Bolognese cuisine, also famous for many other fresh pasta recipes and more. You just need to prepare it and enjoy it with your family for a very special day!

Ingridients

  • Remillid durum wheat semolina 2.1 cups
  • Spinach 8.8 oz
  • Egg yolks 3
  • Flour 00 1.3 cups
  • Eggs 2
  • Beef 10.6 oz (300 g)
  • Pancetta bacon 5.3 oz
  • Extra virgin olive oil 1 tbsp
  • Fine salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Carrots ½ cup (50 g)
  • Celery ½ cup (50 g)
  • Yellow onions 1 cup (50 g)
  • Tomato puree 1 ¼ cup (300 g)
  • Vegetable broth to taste
  • Red wine ½ cup (100 g)
  • Butter 0.3 cup (70 g)
  • Whole milk 1 quart (1 l)
  • Nutmeg to taste
  • Flour 00 0.6 cup (70 g)
  • Fine salt 1 pinch
  • Butter to taste
  • Parmigiano Reggiano DOP cheese 2.3 cups (270 g)

Steps

  1. Brown meat with onions and bell peppers, and season to taste. Add tomato paste and water. Let simmer.
  2. In a mixing bowl, mix ricotta cheese with beaten egg. Set aside.
  3. Preheat oven to 370 degrees F (190 degrees C). Butter a 9 x 13 inch baking dish.
  4. Begin layering all ingredients beginning with a few spoonfuls of tomato sauce. Follow with noodles, then ricotta mixture, and shredded mozzarella. Repeat until dish is filled.
  5. Bake at 375 degrees F (190 degrees C) about 35 to 45 minutes, until bubbly. Let cool a couple of minutes before cutting.
